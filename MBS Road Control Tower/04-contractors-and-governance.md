# 04 · Contractors & Governance

> **Why this matters:** The April 2026 report names **lack of a single supervising authority** as the *first* of four root-cause challenges. The corridor is delivered by 10 contractors — 6 government-internal teams and 4 private-sector vendors — and leadership needs a single view of who is performing, who is slipping, and who needs intervention.

## Why governance is a root cause

Per the report (slide 9), corridor work today touches:

- **4 contractors** in maintenance
- **7 contractors** in infrastructure projects
- **3 contractors** in studies
- **4 contractors** (incl. private sector) in commercial / concession projects

This fragmentation creates predictable failure modes:

1. **No single owner for end-to-end outcomes.** Pilgrim-experience targets like "the corridor is clean and welcoming for Hajj" fall between RGA (road), HCM (street), GDT (traffic posts), and TIP (greenery). When something slips, accountability diffuses.
2. **Coordination tax on every multi-contractor item.** F-Z70-009 (façade obstructing welcome signage) is *blocked* because the fix requires HCM enforcement, owner notice, and possibly RGA/GDT sign-off.
3. **Duplicated studies, inconsistent standards.** Different contractors commission separate studies for adjacent assets, producing incompatible specs.
4. **No unified operational view.** Leadership cannot ask "what is the status of MBS Road this week — including vendor performance?" and get one answer.

---

## The proposed fix — Joint Working Room

The report recommends standing up an **independent program** with three properties:

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Lead** | MoMAH (single accountable owner) |
| **Members** | HCM (Makkah side) + JM (Jeddah side), plus standing seats for RGA, GDT, GDCD, and the private contractors (ARC, SBG, MAAS, TIP) |
| **Mandate** | Authority over findings remediation, **contractor performance review**, contractor escalation, single weekly status report to leadership |

**Operating model:** weekly cadence, shared findings backlog, vendor scorecard reviewed monthly, escalation SLA for blocked items (target: any blocked item resolved in the room ≤ 10 days).

---

## Implication for the agent

The agent must:

- Speak **in the language of the joint room**, not in contractor silos
- For every finding, surface the **executing contractor** alongside status and severity (so leadership knows whom to call)
- Highlight **blocked items where the blocker is a contractor hand-off** (these are exactly what the room exists to resolve)
- Flag **vendors with deteriorating performance** before they become escalations
